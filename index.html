Welcome to the World Cup 2030


Morocco, Portugal, and Spain have been officially designated as hosts of the 2030 FIFA World Cup by the Congress of the International Federation of Association Football (FIFA), which convened on Wednesday in an extraordinary session via videoconference.


During this session, chaired by FIFA President Gianni Infantino from Zurich, the Congress members also approved the allocation of the three centennial matches in 2030 to Uruguay, Argentina, and Paraguay, while Saudi Arabia was confirmed as the host nation for the 2034 World Cup.



The three bids were unanimously approved by the 211 FIFA member association s, who voted separately on the designation procedure adopted by FIFA and the respective bids. In a speech on this occasion, Mr. Infantino stated, "In today’s divided world (...) being able to agree on something like this represents an extraordinary message."


"We live unity. We live inclusivity. We live football," he solemnly declared. For his part, the President of the Royal Moroccan Football Federation (FRMF), Fouzi Lekjaa, expressed his gratitude to the FIFA member associations for making possible “this moment of shared happiness, a moment significant in many ways, starting with its universal representation, rarely seen or experienced in other domains.



"I thank you for placing your trust in my country, Morocco, and its two partners, Portugal and Spain, to jointly organize the 2030 FIFA World Cup," he said in a pre-recorded speech broadcast during the Congress.


"This trust placed in my country by selecting its bid as part of the tripartite candidacy once again reflects the progress made both in the preparations specific to the event and in the overall development of the country, guided by the enlightened vision of His Majesty King Mohammed VI, may God assist Him," Mr. Lekjaa emphasized.



"This will not only contribute to the successful organization of the event but also to the realization of what we have always advocated—that sport, in general, and football, in particular, serve as a lever for socio-economic and human development," he continued.


"The tripartite bid will secure a significant place in history. This is the first time this global event is being held simultaneously in Africa, the cradle of humanity, and on the Old Continent, Europe," concluded the FRMF President.



Moreover, a representative from the independent auditor (BDO), tasked by FIFA with verifying the compliance of bids for the 2030 and 2034 World Cups, concluded that both bidding procedures were conducted with "objectivity, integrity, and transparency."


As the sole candidate for the 2030 World Cup, the Morocco-Portugal-Spain bid, "Yalla Vamos 2030," achieved a major milestone at the end of November with the publication of FIFA's evaluation report. The report highlighted that this tripartite bid is characterized by "its overall quality" and exceeds "the minimum organizational requirements" specified in the technical evaluation, earning a score of 4.2 out of 5.



This strength is evident in the technical evaluation, which covers both infrastructure (sporting and otherwise) and commercial potential, as explained in the report. The Morocco-Portugal-Spain 2030 bid proposes a diverse and appealing selection of stadiums, numbering 20 in total: six in Morocco, three in Portugal, and 11 in Spain.


This surpasses the minimum requirements (14 stadiums) defined for the 2030 World Cup, offering enough flexibility to select iconic and diverse venues across the three countries. The report notes that stadiums such as Santiago Bernabéu in Madrid, Camp Nou in Barcelona, and the Grand Stade Hassan II in Casablanca have been proposed for the opening and final matches.

Participating Teams And Stadiums

Morocco



Casablanca: A new stadium, the Grand Stade Hassan II, with a capacity of 115,000 seats, is under construction in Benslimane near Casablanca, set to open in 2028.


Rabat: The Prince Moulay Abdellah Stadium (69,500 capacity) will host matches and may also host the 2025 Africa Cup of Nations final.


Tangier: The renovated Ibn Batouta Stadium is among the selected venues.


Marrakech: The Marrakech Stadium will host World Cup games.


Agadir: The Stade Adrar is included in the list of venues.


Fès: The Fès Stadium is also part of the selected sites.


Portugal



Lisbon: The Estádio da Luz is selected for World Cup games.


Porto: The Estádio do Dragão is included as one of the host stadiums.


Spain



Madrid: The Santiago Bernabéu Stadium is proposed for key matches, including the opening or final.


Barcelona: Camp Nou is planned for high-profile games.


Bilbao: San Mamés Stadium is among the host venues.


Seville: La Cartuja Stadium will host matches.


Valencia: The Nou Mestalla, a 70,000-seat stadium under construction, is expected to be completed by 2027 and will host matches.


Málaga: La Rosaleda Stadium is being renovated to meet FIFA standards.


La Coruña: Riazor Stadium is included in the host list.


San Sebastián: Anoeta Stadium will host games.


Zaragoza: La Romareda Stadium is also set to host matches.


Latest News

Official award of the 2030 World Cup

FIFA has officially awarded the hosting of the 2030 World Cup to the trio of Spain-Portugal-Morocco. This edition will be unique, taking place on three continents, with the first three matches played in South America (Uruguay, Argentina and Paraguay) to celebrate the centenary of the first World Cup in 1930.

Strengthening security cooperation

On January 29, 2025, Abdellatif Hammouchi, Director General of National Security and Territorial Surveillance of Morocco, met in Madrid with his Spanish and German counterparts. This meeting aimed to strengthen security cooperation in anticipation of the joint organization of the 2030 World Cup.

Call for mobilization of the Moroccan private sector

Fouzi Lekjaa, President of the Royal Moroccan Football Federation, recently urged the General Confederation of Moroccan Enterprises (CGEM) to be actively involved in the preparation of the 2030 World Cup. He stressed the importance of private sector participation to ensure the success of this historic event.
